The Platform · Sign to endorse

They keep writing the same post. Marketing is a cost center. Fold it under sales. An agent can do it. Fine—let them campaign on that. We’ll campaign on our record.

Vote Marketing is not a rebuttal. We’re done debating people who have never run a campaign in their lives. We’re running instead, out loud, on the work: revenue moved, categories created, rooms won, campaigns people still quote a year later.

Here’s what the efficiency crowd keeps missing. The only things that make a company matter are the story people repeat and the experience they actually have. Both are made by humans with taste. We use AI every single day. We’re just not defined by it.

Driven by the tools. Led by taste. No model has ever sat across from a customer and felt the exact second a story lands.

So no, we’re not defending the function. The function is doing fine. We’re running on it, loudly, in public, until the ballot is too long to argue with. This is for every marketer who refuses to be an afterthought—team, IC, or leader. Not because you’re scared of becoming one. Because you were never going to be one.
